Tonale.

Edition.

The primary source for this edition is the facsimile copy of Sion College L1. For each mode, the tonary presents a characteristic melody, then the neumae for the canticles, followed by the differentiae. Finally, using one of the differentiae, the tone is demonstrated using the first verse from the eigth psalm, and the first verses of the Magnificat and Benedictus. The edition adheres to this format.

A secondary source is to be found in DUL Cosin. Ms. V.I.2, where the format of presentation is different. Shown below is a transcription of the first mode exactly as it appears in the source. Most notably, the melodies that characterize each mode are omitted. Instead, the psalm tone is demonstrated first, using the same verse and with the first differentiae. Then follows the remaining differentiae and the neuma. Finally, the Benedictus and Magnificat, in reverse order to the Sion MS.
